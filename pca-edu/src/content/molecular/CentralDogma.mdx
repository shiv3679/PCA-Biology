# The Central Dogma — DNA → RNA → Protein

> “Information flows from stable storage to a working copy,  
>  then to the molecular machines that keep a fly alive.”

---

## 1 Why should a Drosophila lab still care? 🪰

* **Cloning a new CRISPR donor**? — you’re editing DNA.  
* **In-situ staining**? — you’re chasing pre-mRNA splicing patterns.  
* **Proteomics of flight muscle**? — you’re reading the final layer.  
Knowing the hand-offs between these layers lets you troubleshoot at the
right step—not every weird phenotype is transcriptional!

---

## 2 Zoom out: the three hand-offs

| Layer | Key event | Core enzymes / factors |
|-------|-----------|------------------------|
| **DNA** | Replication & repair | DNA-pol δ / ε, RPA, ligase |
| **RNA** | Transcription & splicing | RNA-pol II, spliceosome, CBC |
| **Protein** | Translation | Ribosome, tRNAs, release factors |

---

## 3 DNA — the long-term archive 📚

### 3.1  Replication in the fly embryo  
Early *Drosophila* embryos blast through S phase every **8–10 min**.
The rapid cycles rely on:

* **Helicase MCM2-7** — opens the fork.  
* **DNA-pol ε** — leading-strand synthesis.  
* **PCNA** — sliding clamp that tethers polymerase.

<Callout type="warn">
In those rapid cycles, **no G1/G2 checkpoints** run; replication errors can
sneak through and create somatic mosaics seen later in imaginal discs.
</Callout>

### 3.2  Repair pathways  
* Double-strand breaks: **non-homologous end joining** (Ku70/80, Lig4).  
* Template errors: **MMR** via Msh2/Mlh1.  
* UV lesions: **nucleotide excision repair** (XPC, TFIIH).

---

## 4 RNA — the working copy 📝

### 4.1  Transcription flow  
1. **Promoter melting** — TBP + TFIIB load **RNA-pol II**.  
2. **Elongation checkpoint** — P-TEFb phosphorylates Ser2 of the CTD.  
3. **Termination** — CPSF cuts; **poly-A polymerase** adds the tail.

<Callout type="tip">
*Fly fact:* the 3′ UTR of **even-skipped** adds shadow enhancers in trans;
a long poly-A tail can double protein output in nuclear cycles 13-14.
</Callout>

### 4.2  Splicing & isoforms  
Alternative exons in *Dscam* can generate **38 016** isoforms—
crucial for neuronal wiring.

```python
# Quick Py snippet: translate an exon combination
from Bio.Seq import Seq
isoform = Seq("ATG...TAG")     # paste exon combo
print(isoform.translate())
```

### 4.3 RNA quality checks
- **Nonsense-mediated decay** — UPF proteins degrade PTC-containing mRNAs.

- **piRNA silencing** — protects germ-line from transposons.

## 5 Protein — the end-product 🧬

### 5.1 Translation checkpoints
- **Initiation** — eIF2-GTP brings Met-tRNA.

- **Elongation** — eEF1A loads aminoacyl-tRNA.

- **Termination** — eRF1 recognises stop codon; eRF3 hydrolyses.

Ribosome profiling in Drosophila S2 cells shows stall sites at proline
codons—factor into codon-optimised transgenes.

### 5.2 Post-translational tweaks

| Modification    | Enzyme      | Example function                    |
| --------------- | ----------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Phosphorylation | MAPK        | Torso pathway in early embryo       |
| Ubiquitination  | SCF complex | Cubitus interruptus degradation     |
| Acetylation     | CBP         | Chromatin opening during heat-shock |

## 6 Regulation layers — more than a straight arrow 🔄
- 1. **Epigenetics** — H3K27me3 silences Ubx in wing imaginal disc.

- 2. **miRNAs** — bantam miRNA represses pro-apoptotic hid.

- 3. **Feedback loops** — Ftz-F1 protein boosts its own promoter, sharpening stripes.

<Callout type="info"> The “dogma” is **directional for information**, but regulation flows backwards: proteins tweak transcription; RNAs guide chromatin remodelers. </Callout>

## Troubleshooting cheat-sheet 🛠️

| Symptom                          | Likely layer                   | First fix                                   |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------- |
| mRNA ↑ but protein flat          | Translation or degradation     | Look at ribosome profiling / Western blot   |
| Protein truncated                | DNA mutation or splicing error | Check exon junction reads; Sanger the locus |
| Nothing expresses from transgene | Chromatin silencing            | Add heterologous promoter or insulator      |


## 8 Take-home ✅

- DNA is the archive, RNA the working draft, protein the machine.

- Each hand-off has guard rails (checkpoints, QC pathways).

- Most fly phenotypes trace to one broken step—PCA, RNA-seq, or mass-spec
tells you which layer to zoom in on.

- Time to move from the “telephone directory” to the operating manual of
your favourite gene! 🪰
